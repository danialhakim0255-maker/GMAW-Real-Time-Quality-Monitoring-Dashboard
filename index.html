📋 Project Overview

This real-time Quality Monitoring Dashboard introduces an automated analytics ecosystem designed for Gas Metal Arc Welding (GMAW). By combining sensor fusion datasets with statistical process charts, the system evaluates mechanical integrity, electro-thermal efficiencies, and dimensional stability inside corporate cyber-physical architectures.

Statistical Control Discrepancy Alert:

Statistical limits evaluation confirms the process is Out of Statistical Control. 23 of the 25 tracking points fall outside the specified control limits (\(LCL = 1533.42^\circ\text{C}\), \(UCL = 1572.76^\circ\text{C}\)). This variation points to systemic issues, likely caused by heat buildup in the plates during continuous production cycles or inconsistent shielding gas flow rates across sample sets.

Electromechanical Structural Assessment:

The sync charts show structural changes during the welding cycle. Initial thermal expansion generates a peak internal force of 145 N at 15 seconds as the system resists expansion stresses. When cooling begins, the force falls back to baseline levels, but the LVDT sensor reveals a permanent dimensional change of 0.48 mm, confirming plastic deformation from high localized heat input.

📝 Global Engineering Conclusions

This multi-sensor dashboard project validates structural monitoring methods for Gas Metal Arc Welding operations. Combining electrical flux tracking, infrared thermal imaging, and linear displacement sensors creates a reliable framework for detecting real-time weld defects and process variations.

Statistical analysis shows strong correlation across electrical arc inputs, providing reliable power control options. However, the pyrometric control chart alerts show systematic thermal control challenges, highlighting the need for active cooling loops or real-time travel speed adjustments to handle heat saturation fields.

The synchronized load and displacement charts provide clear data on permanent material distortion. Measuring a 0.48 mm permanent deformation baseline helps engineers optimize fixture clamp geometries and balance structural constraints against residual stress build-ups in the assembly.
